Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

  1. What is 2FA?: Two-Factor Authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring two forms of verification.

  2. Types of 2FA: Common methods include SMS codes, authenticator apps, hardware tokens, and biometric verification.

  3. Setup Process: Typically involves linking your account to a phone number or an authenticator app.

  4. Authenticator Apps: Use apps like Google Authenticator or Authy for generating time-based codes.

  5. Backup Codes: Save backup codes provided during setup in a secure place; they can help you regain access if you lose your 2FA device.

  6. SMS Verification: While convenient, SMS-based 2FA is less secure than app-based methods due to potential SIM swapping attacks.

  7. Biometric Options: Some services offer biometric 2FA, like fingerprint or facial recognition, for added convenience and security.

  8. Hardware Tokens: Devices like YubiKey provide a physical form of 2FA, offering robust security.

  9. Regular Checks: Periodically review and update your 2FA settings to ensure they are still secure and functional.

  10. Recovery Options: Ensure you have recovery options set up, such as an alternate email or phone number, in case you lose access to your primary 2FA method.
