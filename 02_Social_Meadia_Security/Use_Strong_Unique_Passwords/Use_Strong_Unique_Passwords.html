Use Strong, Unique Passwords

  1. Length Matters: Aim for at least 12-16 characters. The longer, the better.

  2. Mix It Up: Use a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

  3. Avoid Personal Info: Don’t use easily guessable information like birthdays, names, or common words.

  4. Unique for Each Account: Never reuse passwords across different accounts.

  5. Randomness is Key: Avoid predictable patterns or sequences like “1234” or “abcd”.

  6. Passphrases: Consider using a series of unrelated words to create a memorable but strong passphrase.

  7. Password Managers: Use a password manager to generate and store complex passwords securely.

  8. Regular Updates: Change your passwords periodically, especially for sensitive accounts.

  9. Two-Factor Authentication: Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for an extra layer of security.

  10. Avoid Browser Storage: Don’t save passwords in your web browser as they can be easily accessed by others
