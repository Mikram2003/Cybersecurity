Authentication

  1. Authentication:The process of verifying the identity of a user, device, or entity in a computer system.

  2. Single-Factor Authentication (SFA):Uses one form of verification, such as a password.

  3. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):Requires multiple forms of verification, such as a password and a mobile code.

  4. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):A subset of MFA, typically involving something you know (password) and something you have (a code sent to your phone).

  5. Biometric Authentication:Uses unique biological traits, such as fingerprints or facial recognition, to verify identity.

  6. Token-Based Authentication:Involves a physical device (token) that generates codes for access.

  7. Certificate-Based Authentication:Uses digital certificates issued by a trusted authority to verify identity.

  8. Passwordless Authentication:Relies on methods such as biometrics, tokens, or SMS codes instead of traditional passwords.

  9. OAuth (Open Authorization):A protocol for token-based authentication and authorization on the internet, allowing third-party access without sharing passwords.

  10. SAML (Security Assertion Markup Language):An XML-based framework for exchanging authentication and authorization data between parties.
