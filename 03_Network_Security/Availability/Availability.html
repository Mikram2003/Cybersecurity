Availability

  1. Redundancy:Having multiple systems, networks, or components in place to ensure that if one fails, others can take over without interrupting service.

  2. Fault Tolerance:The ability of a system to continue operating properly in the event of a failure of some of its components.

  3. Load Balancing:Distributing workloads across multiple computing resources to ensure no single resource is overwhelmed and to maintain system availability.

  4. Disaster Recovery:A set of policies and procedures to enable the recovery or continuation of vital technology infrastructure after a natural or human-induced disaster.

  5. High Availability (HA):Systems and components that are continuously operational and have minimal downtime.

  6. Failover:Switching to a redundant or standby system upon the failure or abnormal termination of the currently active system.

  7. Backups:Regular copying of data to an offsite location to ensure it can be restored in case of data loss or corruption.

  8. Scalability:The capability of a system to handle increased load by adding resources, ensuring continued availability as demand grows.

  9. Network Security Monitoring:Continuous monitoring of network traffic to detect and respond to potential threats that could impact availability.

  10. Service Level Agreements (SLAs):Contracts with service providers that define the expected level of service, including availability, and remedies for failure to meet those expectations.
