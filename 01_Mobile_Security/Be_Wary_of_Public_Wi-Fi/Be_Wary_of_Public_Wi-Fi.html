Be Wary Of Public Wi-Fi

  1. Unencrypted Networks: Many public Wi-Fi networks are unencrypted, making it easy for hackers to intercept your data.

  2. Malicious Hotspots: Cybercriminals can set up fake Wi-Fi networks that mimic legitimate ones to steal your information.

  3. Man-in-the-Middle Attacks: Hackers can intercept communications between you and the websites you visit, capturing sensitive data.

  4. Malware Distribution: Connecting to public Wi-Fi can expose your device to malware, which can be installed without your knowledge.

  5. Snooping and Sniffing: Hackers can use special software to eavesdrop on your online activities on public Wi-Fi.

  6. Session Hijacking: Attackers can hijack your session and gain access to your accounts by stealing session cookies.

  7. Data Theft: Personal information, such as login credentials and financial details, can be stolen while using public Wi-Fi.

  8. Ransomware Attacks: Your device can be infected with ransomware, which locks your files until a ransom is paid.

  9. Identity Theft: Hackers can use stolen information to impersonate you and commit fraud.

  10. Business Email Compromise: Attackers can gain access to business accounts and use them for fraudulent activities.
