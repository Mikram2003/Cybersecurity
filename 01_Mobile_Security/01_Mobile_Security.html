Use Strong Passwords and Biometrics

Use a mix of letters, numbers, and special characters, and aim for at least 12 characters.

Keep Software Updated

Updates often include patches for security vulnerabilities that have been discovered since the last version. Keeping your software updated helps protect your system from malware, viruses, and other cyber threats.

Be Wary of Public Wi-Fi

Many public Wi-Fi networks are unencrypted, meaning data transmitted over them can be easily intercepted by cybercriminals. This makes it easier for hackers to access your personal information.

Monitor App Permissions

Apps often request access to personal data such as contacts, location, and photos. Monitoring permissions helps ensure that apps only access the information they need, reducing the risk of identity theft and privacy breaches.
