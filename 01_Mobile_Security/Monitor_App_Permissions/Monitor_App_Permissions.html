Monitor App Permissions

  1. Review Permissions Regularly: Periodically check the permissions granted to your apps to ensure they align with their functionality.

  2. Use Permission Manager: Utilize the built-in permission manager on your device to see which apps have access to sensitive data like your camera, microphone, and location.

  3. Limit Permissions: Only grant permissions that are necessary for the app to function. Deny permissions that seem excessive or unrelated to the app’s purpose.

  4. Check App Updates: After updating apps, review their permissions again as updates can sometimes request new permissions.

  5. Be Wary of New Apps: When installing new apps, carefully review the permissions they request before granting them.

  6. Use Privacy Dashboard: On Android 12 and later, use the Privacy Dashboard to see which apps have accessed your data in the past 24 hours.

  7. Disable Unnecessary Permissions: If an app requests access to data it doesn’t need, disable those permissions in your settings.

  8. Monitor Background Activity: Some apps may access your data in the background. Use your device’s settings to monitor and control this activity.

  9. Check for Suspicious Activity: Be on the lookout for apps that request permissions that seem suspicious or unnecessary for their functionality.

  10. Educate Yourself: Stay informed about the types of permissions apps can request and the potential risks associated with granting them.
