Encryption

  1. Definition: The process of converting information into a secure format that cannot be read without a decryption key.

  2. Symmetric Encryption: Uses the same key for both encryption and decryption, such as AES (Advanced Encryption Standard).

  3. Asymmetric Encryption: Uses a pair of keysâ€”a public key for encryption and a private key for decryption, such as RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman).

  4. End-to-End Encryption: Ensures data is encrypted on the sender's device and only decrypted on the recipient's device.

  5. Encryption Algorithms: Includes algorithms like AES, RSA, and DES (Data Encryption Standard).

  6. Encryption in Transit: Protects data as it travels across networks, ensuring it cannot be intercepted and read by unauthorized parties.

  7. Encryption at Rest: Protects data stored on devices or servers, ensuring it cannot be accessed if the physical device is compromised.

  8. Key Management: Involves the creation, distribution, storage, and destruction of encryption keys to maintain security.

  9. SSL/TLS: Protocols that use encryption to secure communication over the internet.

  10. Data Privacy: Encryption helps ensure data privacy by making information unreadable to unauthorized users.
