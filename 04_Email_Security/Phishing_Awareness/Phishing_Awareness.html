Phishing Awareness

  1. Definition: Understanding and recognizing attempts to deceive individuals into providing sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity.

  2. Email Phishing: Fraudulent emails pretending to be from reputable sources to steal personal information.

  3. Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing attacks aimed at specific individuals or organizations.

  4. Whaling: Phishing attacks directed at senior executives and other high-profile targets within an organization.

  5. Vishing: Phishing attacks conducted via phone calls, often involving fraudulent claims or requests for sensitive information.

  6. Smishing: Phishing attacks using SMS messages to trick individuals into divulging personal information.

  7. Signs of Phishing: Recognizing common indicators such as generic greetings, urgent language, and suspicious links or attachments.

  8. Education and Training: Providing regular training sessions to help employees identify and respond to phishing attempts.

  9. Verification: Encouraging users to verify the authenticity of requests for sensitive information through trusted channels.

  10. Reporting: Establishing procedures for reporting suspected phishing attempts to IT or security teams.
